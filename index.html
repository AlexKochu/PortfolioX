Product Decision

Chose Random Forest over a black-box deep-learning model because explainability -- showing users why a path was recommended -- was identified as the primary drop-off cause. Accuracy (94%) was a constraint, not the goal.

Most career guidance tools give the same top-5 roles to every user. The real gap was personalisation at the skill-gap level, not the job-title level.

Reframe: "Which skills does this specific user need to become hireable for role X?"

Random Forest classifier (94% accuracy) combined with K-Means clustering to group users by skill archetype before recommending. NLP pipeline normalised 50+ self-reported skill variations into canonical labels.

Output: Ranked career paths with confidence scores + skill-gap delta per user

Designed a composite index surfacing the exact skill gaps keeping a user from a target role. Generates a personalised upskilling roadmap with prioritised learning steps.

PM Case Study
WhatsApp -- Priority Notifications
Improving Engagement for College Students in High-Volume Group Chats
2026

End-to-end product thinking exercise following the 8-step PM framework -- from goal-setting through root cause diagnosis, solution prioritisation, and final recommendation with tradeoff rationale.

8
PM Framework Steps
4
Solutions Evaluated
1
Recommended Build
Key Insight

The core problem is information retrieval, not messaging volume. Students aren't overwhelmed by messages -- they can't surface the ones that matter. This reframe drove the entire solution space.

Target: college students in 5-15 simultaneous group chats. Goal: reduce missed critical messages without creating notification fatigue or violating privacy expectations.

North Star: % of users who read a high-priority message within 15 min of send

Every message carries equal visual weight regardless of importance. No mechanism for senders or recipients to signal urgency. The platform's UI treats a "class cancelled" alert identically to a meme.

Root cause: Information retrieval failure, not messaging volume

Evaluated: Priority Notifications (AI-flagged), Smart Digest, Message Pinning 2.0, and Quiet Hours. Scored on user impact, implementation complexity, and privacy risk.

Priority Notifications wins: highest impact / effort ratio, least privacy surface area

Priority Notifications emerged as the best initial approach due to its balance of measurable engagement impact, manageable implementation effort, and respect for end-to-end encryption privacy constraints.

Strongest PM answers are grounded in clear goals, precise root causes, and honest tradeoffs
